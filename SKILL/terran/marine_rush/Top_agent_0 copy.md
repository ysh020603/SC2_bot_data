# Summary

All-in Marine rush: mineral-only economy capped at 20 SCVs, 6 Barracks producing Marines non-stop. Skip gas, skip second base, launch a 20-Marine zone attack across the map. Designed to end the game in the early phases.

# Details

Your overall strategy is a highly aggressive Marine all-in rush, with a strict emphasis on sacrificing long-term macro development to overwhelm the opponent with an early, massive tier-one infantry swarm:

* Opening & Economy: Start with a Supply Depot at 14 supply. As soon as the depot finishes, construct your first Barracks. Keep this a strictly mineral-only economy (do not take any gas Refineries). Halt SCV production exactly at 20 workers to channel all available resources directly into your army.
* Production Escalation: Once the first Barracks is ready, morph your Command Center into an Orbital Command to utilize MULEs for a rapid mineral boost. Build a second Supply Depot to support your population cap, then immediately drop down 5 additional Barracks (scaling up to a total of 6 Barracks) to maximize your production capacity.
* Scouting & Utility: Send out an early worker scout as soon as your first Supply Depot begins. Rely on MULE drop-downs to sustain your massive 6-Barracks production cycle, and save energy for Orbital Scans after the 5-minute mark if you need vision or detection.
* Expansion & Tactics: Do not expand to a second base. Gather your steadily growing army safely at your natural expansion. Once your army reaches exactly 20 Marines, launch your all-in zone attack across the map.
* Micro Maneuvers (Dodge Ramp): Pay close attention to enemy ramp defenses. If the opponent (Protoss) casts Forcefields to block their main ramp, your forces must execute a tactical retreat back to your natural gather point to avoid being trapped or out-ranged, resuming the push only once the path is clear.
* End Goal: Continually stream Marines non-stop out of your 6 Barracks (queueing up to 200 Marines if the game drags on) to completely overrun the enemy base and end the game decisively in the early phases.
