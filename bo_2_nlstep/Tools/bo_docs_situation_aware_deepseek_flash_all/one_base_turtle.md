# Summary

Open depot-first, rax into factory with a tech lab on the factory for siege tank production, and you stay on one base with an early Orbital Command while dropping multiple bunkers for a turtle defense. You keep pumping marines from a couple of rax and tanks from the factory, stacking up a bio-mech force with no early expansion or upgrades, just pure defensive macro and steady production. The endgame is a maxed-out one-base army of marines and siege tanks, relying on overwhelming sustained production and positional defense to hold the opponent, with no plan to expand or transition to late-game tech.

# Details

[Step 1] Start by throwing down a depot to keep supply flowing, then keep SCV production going steadily to saturate the mineral line while you drop a rax and a refinery to set up your early economy and tech capability. Add another depot to maintain a healthy supply buffer as you continue training workers and start a marine for initial defense.

[Step 2] Morph the Command Center into an Orbital Command while keeping SCV and Marine production going as the economy still has room to grow. Add a Factory to transition into tech and throw down a pair of Bunkers for a solid defensive posture, along with a Refinery to strengthen gas income for the upcoming tech path. Make sure depot production keeps pace with the steady stream of workers and Marines so supply doesn't stall.

[Step 3] Drop the Factory and start a tech lab on it, then begin producing a Siege Tank while keeping SCV production steady to saturate the economy. Add an extra rax alongside the Factory tech lab, reinforce with Marines, and throw down a depot to keep supply headroom comfortable for the growing mineral and gas demand.

[Step 4] Add another rax to scale bio production, then pump out a few Marines while the Factory starts rolling out Siege Tanks. Keep the supply headroom comfortable with a couple of depots, and ensure the Refinery count supports the gas-hungry tank production as the mech backbone comes online.

[Step 5] Ramp up army production by training a wave of Marines from the Barracks while the Factory starts producing Siege Tanks for positional defense. Add a Supply Depot to keep headroom comfortable for the growing bio-mech force, and reinforce with another Tank to strengthen the core army composition.

[Step 6] Keep pumping out Marines from the Barracks while adding a Siege Tank from the Factory for some early firepower. Throw down depots as needed to keep supply headroom comfortable for the growing bio force, and make sure the gas income is healthy enough to support the tank production.

[Step 7] Pump out a steady wave of Marines from the rax while the factory produces Siege Tanks, reinforcing the bio-mech core to build up a solid mid-game fighting force. Keep an eye on supply headroom to drop depots as needed, and ensure gas income is strong enough to support the tank production without stalling mineral income.

[Step 8] Ramp up bio production by pumping out a wave of Marines from the Barracks while the Factory adds Siege Tanks for defensive power, and throw down a depot to keep supply headroom comfortable for the growing army and worker count.

[Step 9] Pump out a wave of Marines from the rax while the factory produces a couple of Siege Tanks, building up a solid bio-tank composition. Keep an eye on supply headroom to throw down depots as needed, and make sure the gas income is strong enough to support the tank production without stalling the mineral-heavy Marine reinforcement.

[Step 10] Throw down a pair of depots to make room for the growing bio force, while pumping out a steady stream of Marines from the rax. Tech up by adding a couple of Siege Tanks from the factory, giving the army some much-needed defensive punch and siege potential. Keep the gas income healthy to support these tank builds without stalling the marine production.

[Step 11] This is an ultra-committal one-base turtle defense that banks on pure bio-mech attrition, using bunkers and siege tank positioning to hold while maxing out on a single-base economy. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline -- adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
