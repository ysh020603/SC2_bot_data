# Summary

Opens depot-first into a single Rax that gets an Orbital upgrade, with no expansion or gas taken, just pumping pure Marines from a growing number of Barracks. Mid-game sees mass Rax count climb to 3 and then 4, with non-stop Marine production and constant depots to avoid supply blocks, relying entirely on bio for map presence and defense. Late-game aims for a relentless flood of Marines off a one-base economy, using overwhelming numbers and sustained production to overrun the opponent before any tech or expansion is needed.

# Details

[Step 1] Start the game by training several SCVs and adding a depot to keep up with the growing supply demand. Then drop a rax and continue worker production while adding additional depots and rax to set up a strong early production foundation.

[Step 2] Morph the starting Command Center into an Orbital Command for the MULE and supply drop utility, while keeping SCV production rolling as the economy still has room to saturate. Add two more Barracks to ramp up bio production, and train a handful of Marines from the existing and new rax for early map presence and defense. Make sure depot construction keeps pace with the growing marine count and worker production to avoid blocking supply.

[Step 3] Throw down an extra depot to keep supply headroom comfortable, then queue up a steady stream of Marines from the existing rax while adding another Barracks to scale up bio production. Keep SCV production humming to saturate the mineral line, balancing worker count with the growing army demand.

[Step 4] Pump out a wave of Marines from the Barracks while adding depots to keep up with the growing supply demand.

[Step 5] Pump out a wave of Marines from the rax and drop a depot to keep supply ahead of the growing production demand.

[Step 6] Pump out a wave of 10 Marines from the Barracks to build up a solid infantry force, keeping an eye on supply headroom so you can add depots as needed to avoid getting blocked.

[Step 7] Pump out a steady wave of Marines from the Barracks to build up a solid bio core, and throw down a depot to keep supply ahead of the growing army.

[Step 8] Throw down 3 depots to keep supply headroom comfortable for the upcoming Marine production cycle, and pump out 7 Marines from the rax to build up the bio force.

[Step 9] Pump out a wave of Marines from the rax to build up the bio army, and throw down a depot to keep supply headroom comfortable as the Marine count climbs.

[Step 10] This is a pure all-in Marine flood that relies on overwhelming the opponent with sheer numbers from a one-base economy, sacrificing tech and expansion for relentless early pressure. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline -- adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
