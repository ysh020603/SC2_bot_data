# Summary

Open with gas first and a quick Reaper to scout, followed by a Factory making Hellions and a Starport with a Tech Lab for a Raven, then drop your natural CC around the same time as you start a Cyclone and Siege Tank for early map control. Scale up with a pile of Barracks on reactors, pump Marines with Stim and Combat Shield, mix in Tanks, Vikings, and Medivacs for drop harassment or a timing push, and double Engineering Bay for +1/+1 while taking a third. Transition into a Liberator-heavy bio-mech deathball with +2/+2 and mech upgrades from the Armory, aiming to overwhelm with sustained Marine–Tank–Viking–Liberator production off three bases.

# Details

[Step 1] Start by throwing down a Supply Depot and the first Refinery to get gas flowing early, then drop the Barracks, followed by the second Refinery—all while constantly producing SCVs to keep your economy humming for the early game macro phase.

[Step 2] After upgrading the Command Center to an Orbital Command, kick off Factory production and immediately train a Reaper. Throw down two depots to avoid supply blocks, keep pumping workers from the orbital, then train a second Reaper and start producing a Hellion for early pressure.

[Step 3] Drop a fresh Command Center while keeping your SCV line running, then produce a Hellion and a Reaper for map presence. Add a depot, build your Starport, and attach Tech Labs to both the Factory and the rax. Train a Cyclone and a Raven while pumping out several more SCVs to saturate your new base.

[Step 4] Morph the first Command Center into an Orbital Command for Mule energy, start producing a Siege Tank from the Factory for positional defense, and send out a group of SCVs to take a natural expansion with a new Command Center. Drop a Refinery at the expansion gas, add a Supply Depot to avoid supply blocks, and keep worker production steady by training multiple SCVs from both bases.

[Step 5] Continue pumping SCVs from the main base while adding a Reactor onto one of the Barracks to double up Marine production, and train a Siege Tank from the Factory for positional defense. Also build a Raven from the Starport for detection or interference, and throw down an extra Supply Depot to keep the production queue flowing.

[Step 6] Drop a couple of depots to stay ahead on supply while keeping SCV production going, then start outputting a Siege Tank and two Vikings from the Factory and Starport for a sturdy midgame army composition, followed by another depot and worker to maintain macro momentum.

[Step 7] Add two more Barracks to ramp up bio production, keep worker production humming from the main, morph the Command Center into an Orbital Command for faster income and scans, then start churning out a couple of Vikings and a Siege Tank to gain map control and hold any early aggression.

[Step 8] Drop two Engineering Bays for double upgrades, keep pumping workers from your Command Centers, then take three more Refineries to secure a gas-heavy income. Start researching Stimpack and immediately begin building a Reactor on your Barracks for mass Marine production.

[Step 9] Keep SCV production humming while adding a reactor to the rax for double marine production, throw down a few depots to avoid supply blocks, start the first two Vikings out of the starport for map vision or Viking control, and begin researching +1 infantry attack to give your bio a damage edge.

[Step 10] Throw down two more Barracks and a Supply Depot to keep up with macro, continue worker production from the Command Center, then from the Factory produce a Siege Tank, and start churning out a handful of Marines to build up a push.

[Step 11] Keep worker production steady by training several SCVs from the Command Center, reinforce with two Marines and a Medivac, and add a few supply depots to avoid getting supply blocked.

[Step 12] Keep SCV production going and throw down a couple of depots to avoid supply blocks, while massing several Marines and adding a Medivac for a drop or reinforcement. Research infantry armor to boost your bio's survivability, and produce a Siege Tank to start building a solid mech backbone for defense or a timing push.

[Step 13] Build a second rax and attach a reactor to it for rapid Marine production, while your Starport produces two Medivacs for drop potential; throw down a couple of depots to keep supply ahead.

[Step 14] Throw down an Armory to unlock mech upgrades, while massing a few Marines and producing a Siege Tank to bolster your army, and add three depots to keep supply from blocking production.

[Step 15] Start by producing a Liberator from the starport, then pump out a wave of Marines — about half a dozen — while adding a couple of Supply Depots to avoid supply blocks. Don't forget to research Combat Shield to give your infantry a bit more survivability.

[Step 16] Issue a Liberator from the Starport for zone control, add two depots to keep supply open, and equip a Barracks with a Reactor to double Marine production—pump out a handful of Marines while also rolling out a Siege Tank from the Factory.

[Step 17] Kick off bio +1 and mech +1 upgrades, then pump out a solid group of Marines and a Viking for air support and map vision.

[Step 18] Keep pumping several Marines from your rax while building a Viking for early map control and a supply depot to avoid getting blocked. Drop your first Factory and start Siege Tank production to strengthen your position.

[Step 19] Continue churning out marines from your rax to reinforce the main army, add a couple of depots to keep supply flowing, and then produce a viking from the starport for air control or potential drop support.

[Step 20] Continue massing Marines and start researching infantry armor for durability, while building a Viking to provide air support and map vision.

[Step 21] Start by training a Siege Tank from the Factory, then queue up a wave of Marines from the Barracks for solid ground support. After that, add a Tech Lab onto the Factory to unlock further technology options, produce a Viking from the Starport for air control, and train one final Marine to round out the army.

[Step 22] Add a reactor onto one of the rax to double Marine production, while starting a Viking from the Starport for map vision and anti-air coverage. Keep churning out a solid group of Marines to build up your bio force.

[Step 23] Keep the rax churning out a wave of Marines while the Starport produces a Liberator, then drop a few more Marines before adding a Viking. Finish up this phase by starting mech +1 to strengthen your vehicle and air units, and reinforce with one final Marine.

[Step 24] Maintain constant production from your reactored Barracks and Starport, pumping Marines, Vikings, and Liberators while adding Siege Tanks from your Factory to keep your core composition rolling. If you scout heavy Viking or air dominance from your opponent, prioritize more Vikings and consider a few Thors for anti-air punch; against a mass Tank turtle, use Liberator zones and Medivac drops to force movement or snipe economy. As you secure your third and fourth bases, aim to max out on Marine–Tank–Viking–Liberator, then add a second Starport and Tech Lab to produce Ravens for interference matrix, or transition into Banshees for harassment if you've denied their detection. Upgrade to +2/+2 infantry and +2 mech, and as the game goes late, consider adding Ghosts for EMP against Protoss or Terran energy units, or Battlecruisers for sustained basetrading. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline — adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
