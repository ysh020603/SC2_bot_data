# Summary

You open with a standard 1-rax expand—wait, nope, you actually stay one-base: drop a depot, rax, refinery, then orbital, factory, and two bunkers at the natural for a hard turtle, skipping any expansion and going straight into siege tank production off a tech lab. Mid game you add a second and third rax, keep pumping marines and tanks from the factory, but there’s no mention of stim, shields, or any upgrades—just pure bio-tank volume on one base. Late game you’re still on one base, churning out waves of marines and siege tanks with no tech transition, so the plan is to max out on that low-tech bio-tank composition and either hold a desperate all-in or slowly push out with what you have.

# Details

[Step 1] Start by dropping the first Supply Depot, then keep SCV production steady while you build your first Barracks and Refinery. Add a second Depot to not get supply blocked, train several more SCVs, and finish the phase by starting a Marine for early defense.

[Step 2] Upgrade the first Command Center into an Orbital Command for faster income and scan vision, then start a Marine and begin building a Factory for tech transitions. Keep SCV production going while adding a Supply Depot and a Refinery for gas income. For early defense, throw down two Bunkers at the natural and follow up by training a few more Marines from the Barracks to man them.

[Step 3] With your first Factory now online, keep SCV and Marine production humming along while attaching a couple of Tech Labs to the Factory to start Siege Tank production. Throw down an extra Supply Depot and add a second Barracks to reinforce your army with more Marines as you prepare for a mech-oriented mid-game push.

[Step 4] Add another Barracks to boost your bio production, then pump out a Marine and a Siege Tank, followed by another Marine and a second Tank to solidify your ground force. Drop a Supply Depot to avoid getting supply capped, then train two more Marines, throw down another depot, and finish with one final Marine to keep the pressure up.

[Step 5] Continue pumping several Marines from the rax while adding a depot to keep supply open, and from the factory, produce a couple of Siege Tanks to strengthen your defensive line.

[Step 6] Ramp up marine production from the barracks while rolling out a Siege Tank from the factory for early pressure or defense, and throw down a couple of depots to keep supply from capping out.

[Step 7] Reinforce your army with a handful of Marines and three Siege Tanks, building up a solid bio-mech force for an upcoming push.

[Step 8] Continue producing Marines and Siege Tanks from the Barracks and Factory, while adding another Supply Depot to keep the production flowing without supply blocks.

[Step 9] Pump out a wave of marines from the rax while the factory produces a couple of Siege Tanks to round out the bio-tank composition.

[Step 10] Throw down a couple more depots to keep supply flowing, then start pumping out marines in waves while adding siege tanks from the factory to form a solid bio-tank composition.

[Step 11] Continue producing marines and siege tanks from your existing one-base infrastructure, using your orbital for scans to spot opponent tech; if you scout heavy air, add a starport for vikings, and if you see a fast expansion, consider a slow push with siege tanks. Once you max out on your low-tech composition, taking a natural expansion can sustain your production for a longer game. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline — adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
