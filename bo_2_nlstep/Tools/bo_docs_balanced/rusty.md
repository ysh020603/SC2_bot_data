# Summary

Open with a standard CC-first into Orbital, drop your natural expansion, then go Rax -> Factory with a Tech Lab for Siege Tank production, and add a Starport for early tech access. Mid-game you scale up to 4 Rax with reactors for mass Marine production, keep the Factory on Tech Lab for constant Tanks, and add a Starport for Medivacs and Vikings, while grabbing Combat Shield and dropping a quick third base. In the late game you’re looking at a classic bio–mech snowball, nonstop waves of Marines backed by Siege Tanks and Medivac–Viking support, aiming to overwhelm through sustained production and relentless pressure.

# Details

[Step 1] Start by dropping your first Supply Depot and pumping out several SCVs from the Command Center. Then take your first Refinery for gas, followed by the first Barracks. Keep SCV production constant throughout, and add a second Depot to stay ahead on supply.

[Step 2] Morph the first Command Center into an Orbital to open up scanning, and train a Marine while you start your natural expansion with a second CC. Keep producing SCVs and a couple more Marines, then throw down a Refinery to secure gas income. Follow up by building your first Factory with a Tech Lab attached, and stay on top of supply by adding a Depot.

[Step 3] Upgrade the Command Center to an Orbital Command while maintaining SCV production and a steady flow of Marines from the Barracks. Start building the second Factory to support continuous Siege Tank production, and produce a couple of Tanks to establish a strong defensive line.

[Step 4] Add two refineries for gas income, keep pumping SCVs, and lay down a depot. Attach a Tech Lab to the Factory to start Siege Tank production, and train a Marine for early defense.

[Step 5] Keep SCV production going nonstop while throwing down the first Barracks, then add a Starport for early tech access, and follow up with a second Barracks to double bio output. Drop several Supply Depots to prevent supply blocks as your army starts to grow.

[Step 6] Add the third and fourth Barracks, putting a tech lab on one and a reactor on the Starport, while pushing out a Medivac and a pair of Siege Tanks; research Combat Shield and reinforce the army with several Marines.

[Step 7] While producing two Siege Tanks from the Factory, throw down a few depots to keep the supply flowing, add reactors to the Barracks for a marine-heavy production line, then start a new Command Center to secure a third base.

[Step 8] Continue reinforcing the army by producing a couple of Marines from the Barracks while the Starport pumps out a Medivac and a Viking for air support. The Factory rolls out a pair of Siege Tanks, and to keep the production flowing, throw down several more depots to free up supply.

[Step 9] Produce a wave of Marines from the Barracks while building two Siege Tanks from the Factory and a Medivac from the Starport. Don't forget to add two more Supply Depots to keep the production flowing.

[Step 10] Throw down a depot, then start massing marines from the rax—pump out a solid group of them—and add another depot to keep supply flowing.

[Step 11] Morph the Command Center into an Orbital Command for extra scan and drop support, pump out 2 Medivacs for medivac mobility, add 2 Siege Tanks for siege presence, and reinforce with a wave of Marines to round out the bio-mech composition.

[Step 12] Reinforce with a steady stream of Marines from the rax while the factory produces a couple of Siege Tanks for defensive positioning, and throw in a Viking from the starport for air superiority or high-ground vision.

[Step 13] Produce a wave of Marines from the Barracks to reinforce your army.

[Step 14] Crank out a pair of Siege Tanks from the Factory while the Barracks pump a steady stream of Marines, building up the bio-mech army for an aggressive push.

[Step 15] Keep pumping out a wave of Marines from the rax while your factory produces a Siege Tank for added firepower, and throw down three more depots to ensure you don't get supply blocked as you prepare for the next push.

[Step 16] Throw down several depots to stay ahead on supply, produce a Siege Tank for map presence, and rally a few Marines from the Barracks to bolster your army.

[Step 17] Pump out a wave of Marines from the Barracks while producing a Siege Tank from the Factory, building up a solid bio-mech force for the upcoming engagement.

[Step 18] Keep pumping out a wave of Marines from the Barracks while the Starport builds a Medivac and a Viking to give the army healing mobility and air control.

[Step 19] Push out a wave of Marines from the rax, along with a Siege Tank from the factory and a Medivac from the starport to reinforce your army with both firepower and mobility.

[Step 20] Start building a Medivac for mobility and reinforce with a wave of Marines, pumping out about 9 from the rax to support your next move.

[Step 21] Train a few Marines from the Barracks while dropping multiple depots to keep supply from capping, ensuring continuous production and army reinforcement.

[Step 22] Pump out a large wave of Marines from your Barracks to build up a strong bio army, preparing either for an aggressive timing attack or to reinforce your defensive line.

[Step 23] Continue massing Marines and Siege Tanks from your 4 Rax and Factory while keeping Starport production on Medivacs and Vikings for mobility and air control. If you scout heavy banelings, spread your tanks and consider adding a third Factory for more splash. Against heavy air transitions like mutalisks or void rays, increase Viking count or add Thors from a second Factory. Once you reach 4+ bases, transition into Liberators for zone control and Ghosts for EMP against Protoss. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline — adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
