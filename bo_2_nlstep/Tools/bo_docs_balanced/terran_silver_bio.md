# Summary

Open with a Reaper into fast 1-1-0, dropping your natural CC on time and teching to concussive shells and Combat Shield off a tech lab rax while adding a reactor for marine production. Mid-game, you pump out a constant stream of marines and a few marauders from 5 rax, slapping reactors on most of them to max out your bio count, grabbing a third base and staying on pure MMM with no factory or starport for a relentless pressure style. Late game, you aim to max out on an overwhelming marine-marauder ball, using non-stop production from mass rax and multiple orbitals to sustain a constant stream of bio and never let up on the aggression.

# Details

[Step 1] Start the game by building a Supply Depot and training SCVs continuously. Set down the first Barracks and Refinery, then follow up with a second Barracks and second Refinery to prepare for a standard marine-focused opener with gas income.

[Step 2] Morph the CC into an Orbital Command as you train a Reaper and a few Marines for early pressure. Keep worker production steady, expand to your natural with the second Command Center, and throw down a depot. Set up the rax with a tech lab and then a reactor, and start researching concussive shells to make your bio more effective.

[Step 3] Start building your first Barracks while keeping SCV production constant. Begin training a Marauder and a Marine for early army, add a couple of Supply Depots to avoid getting supply blocked, and continue pumping SCVs to maintain a strong economy.

[Step 4] Start researching Combat Shield while morphing the Command Center into an Orbital Command. Add a tech lab to the barracks and begin producing a mix of marines and marauders for early game defense or pressure, while continuing worker production to keep the economy humming.

[Step 5] Keep churning out workers from the Command Center while reinforcing with a few Marines and a couple of Marauders from the Barracks. Drop a depot to stay ahead on supply and build up a solid bio core for early aggression.

[Step 6] Keep SCV production going to saturate the natural, add two depots to avoid getting supply blocked, and reinforce the bio ball with a couple of Marauders and Marines to maintain map presence and prepare for your next push.

[Step 7] Keep SCV production flowing to support the growing economy, and throw down two depots to avoid getting supply blocked. Meanwhile, keep the rax active by training a mix of marines and marauders to build up a solid bio force.

[Step 8] Keep worker production steady while throwing down a couple of depots to free up supply. From the rax, train a mix of marines and marauders to build an early bio core.

[Step 9] Take a new expansion while reinforcing the bio army by adding two more Barracks, building a depot to stay ahead on supply, and pumping a mix of Marines and Marauders alongside continuous SCV production to support the growing economy.

[Step 10] Ramp up bio production by adding several more barracks -- build the third, fourth, and fifth rax -- while training a mix of a few marines and two marauders. Drop a depot to keep supply flowing, and add reactors to a couple of the rax to accelerate marine production.

[Step 11] Drop two Refineries for gas income, start training a Marauder and a few Marines from the Barracks, then upgrade the Command Center to an Orbital Command while continuing Marine production to reinforce the army.

[Step 12] Train a handful of marines for early pressure and defense, while throwing down two supply depots to prevent supply blocks. Also, start building a reactor on the barracks to double your marine production going forward.

[Step 13] Throw down an extra depot to free up supply, then start pumping out marines from the rax—churn out a large wave of them to build up a solid bio force for the upcoming engagement.

[Step 14] Pump out a wave of Marines from the Barracks to keep your army size growing, and drop two depots in advance to prevent supply blocks.

[Step 15] Keep the Barracks busy churning out Marines while adding a pair of Supply Depots to free up supply, then continue spamming out a wave of Marines and finish with another extra Depot to ensure your production doesn't stall.

[Step 16] Throw down two depots to keep supply open, then start cranking out 8 Marines from the rax to build up a solid bio core for the upcoming push.

[Step 17] Crank out a Marauder from the rax, throw down a few extra depots to keep supply flowing, and then reinforce with a solid wave of Marines to build up your bio army for the upcoming engagement.

[Step 18] Keep churning out marines from the rax while throwing down a couple of depots to ensure you don't get supply blocked.

[Step 19] Continue pumping out a solid wave of Marines to build up your army, while adding a couple of depots to keep supply from capping out.

[Step 20] Secure supply by adding two depots, then start massing a sizable Marine force by training 8 Marines to establish a solid bio army.

[Step 21] Churn out a wave of Marines from the existing Barracks, then add reactors to a couple of them to ramp up production even further.

[Step 22] Keep up constant marine and marauder production from your 5+ rax, using multiple orbital commands to sustain MULEs and scans while dropping depots to avoid supply blocks. If you scout heavy banelings or colossus, consider adding a factory for widow mines or a starport for vikings to deal with splash. Against turtling mech, use your superior mobility to drop and expand while denying bases. As you secure a fourth base, transition into medivacs for drops and lib swap for zone control, then push for a maxed-out timing. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline — adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
