# Summary

Open with a fast natural after the first Rax, then drop a Factory with Tech Lab for early Siege Tanks and a second Rax with Tech Lab to get Combat Shield rolling. Scale up by spamming mass Barracks with Reactors to flood Marines while pumping Siege Tanks from a couple of Factories, grabbing a third base around step 18 to fuel the bio-mech timing. The late-game plan is a maxed-out marine-tank composition on three bases, relying on constant reinforcements, +1/+1 upgrades, and Stim to overwhelm opponents with sustained pressure.

# Details

[Step 1] Start by placing the first depot to support your supply, then train a pair of SCVs before dropping the first Barracks. Follow up with another worker and put down the Refinery, then continuously pump SCVs out of the Command Center to keep your economy humming.

[Step 2] Expand by dropping the natural Command Center, throw down a supply depot, and keep SCV production flowing uninterrupted. Slide in a Refinery and start the Factory for tech, while pumping out a couple of Marines from the Barracks for early defense.

[Step 3] Keep pumping SCVs to bolster your mineral income, throw down several supply depots to stay ahead of the supply cap, and drop a Factory with a Tech Lab to begin Siege Tank production or other mech options.

[Step 4] Keep churning out SCVs from the CC to bolster your economy, while starting Siege Tank production from the Factory. Add a Tech Lab to the Factory to unlock upgrades or advanced units, and throw down a Supply Depot to ensure you don't get supply blocked during this critical macro phase.

[Step 5] Take a gas Refinery to secure vespene, start producing a Siege Tank from the Factory for early map presence or defense, and keep SCV production flowing while tossing down several Supply Depots to prevent supply blocks as you ramp up.

[Step 6] Keep SCV production steady while dropping a second rax with a tech lab, start pumping out a couple of Siege Tanks from the factory, and research Combat Shield. Add another Refinery to ensure enough gas for the upcoming tech and tank production.

[Step 7] Morph both Command Centers into Orbitals, then keep the Barracks and Factory producing nonstop—crank out 4 Siege Tanks and a handful of Marines while adding a couple of Supply Depots to keep your supply from capping.

[Step 8] Start by throwing down the first three rax, then add a pair of depots to free up supply, and follow up by flooding the next five rax to max out your bio production for a big push.

[Step 9] Add two more Barracks with Reactors to pump Marines while continuing Siege Tank production from the Factories, building a solid bio-mech force for map control and midgame engagements.

[Step 10] Drop the first Factory to begin your mech transition while keeping the Barracks active with constant Marine production for map presence. As soon as the Factory finishes, start Siege Tank production — produce two in a row — and continue reinforcing with a steady stream of Marines from the Barracks to support your tank push.

[Step 11] Continue pumping Marines from your Barracks while adding a Tech Lab on the Factory for tech upgrades – you'll also need to throw down several depots in advance to avoid getting supply blocked.

[Step 12] With mineral income to spare, start spamming depots aggressively to free up supply for the upcoming army — drop a full block of 10 depots to ensure you won't get supply blocked during the next production cycle.

[Step 13] Add a depot to keep supply up, then pump out three Siege Tanks from the Factory while reinforcing with several Marines from the Barracks to solidify your position.

[Step 14] Crank out a wave of Marines while adding the first Siege Tank to the army composition, setting up for a strong mid-game timing or to support a defensive position.

[Step 15] Start producing 2 Siege Tanks from the Factory while the Barracks churns out a wave of 8 Marines to reinforce your army for an upcoming push or to secure map control.

[Step 16] Pump out a wave of Marines from the rax while the Factory starts the first Siege Tank, giving you a solid backbone of infantry and some early siege capability.

[Step 17] Pump out 11 Marines from the rax to bulk up the bio force for the next engagement.

[Step 18] Take a new CC for the expansion, morph it into an Orbital Command right away, then churn out over a dozen marines to build up a strong bio presence.

[Step 19] Maintain constant Marine production from your mass Reactor Barracks and keep Siege Tanks rolling out of your Factories, adding Engineers for repair and +1/+1 upgrades as you saturate your third base; if you scout a Zergling-heavy composition, spread your tanks and add a few Hellions or a second Factory for more Siege Tank count, while against Protoss air transitions, pump out a few Vikings or a Thor in place of extra tanks; as you approach a maxed-out 200/200 army, consider adding Liberators for zone control or a Ghost Academy for EMP vs Protoss shields; use this gameplan as your strategic baseline — adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
