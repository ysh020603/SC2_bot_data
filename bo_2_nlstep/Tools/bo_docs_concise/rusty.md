# Summary

Open gas-first into a quick expand, drop an early Factory with Tech Lab for Siege Tanks, then follow up with a Reactored Starport and double Barracks to set up your bio-mech opener. Mid-game, you scale up to 4-5 Barracks with reactors and a Tech Lab for Combat Shield, massing Marine-Tank-Medivac with a Viking for AA, while you grab a third CC to support your sustained pressure. Late-game, you aim for a maxed bio-tank army with heavy Marine production, constant Medivac and Tank reinforcement, and a Viking for air control, relying on overwhelming numbers and defensive positioning rather than any major tech switch.

# Details

[Step 1] Open with a gas-first build: start by throwing down a depot, then queue up several SCVs for continuous worker production, immediately drop the Refinery for early gas income, and follow up with the first Barracks; add another depot as supply demands grow while keeping the SCV train going.

[Step 2] Upgrade the main Command Center to an Orbital while starting your expansion with a second CC. Keep SCV production going and train a couple of Marines from the first Barracks. Throw down a Refinery for gas income, then start building a Factory with a Tech Lab to set up for early tech play. Add a depot to manage supply as you continue worker production.

[Step 3] Morph the first Command Center into an Orbital Command for mules and scans, while keeping SCV production constant. Train a couple of Marines and start producing Siege Tanks from the existing Factory. Add a second Factory to ramp up tank production, and immediately start another Siege Tank from the new factory.

[Step 4] Set up both refineries at the natural to start gas income while continuously pumping SCVs from the Command Center. Train a Marine for early map vision and defense, throw down a Supply Depot to keep supply open, produce a Siege Tank from the Factory, then add a Tech Lab to unlock siege mode and further upgrades.

[Step 5] Transition into Starport tech by dropping a Starport alongside a second Barracks, while continuing steady SCV production and adding multiple depots to handle the growing supply demand.

[Step 6] Drop two additional Barracks and equip one with a Tech Lab while starting Combat Shield research to beef up Marine survivability. At the same time, produce a Medivac for mobility and a pair of Siege Tanks for defensive power, plus train a handful of Marines to reinforce the army. Finally, add a Reactor to the Starport to double future Medivac production.

[Step 7] Start producing a couple of Siege Tanks from the Factory for defensive punch, while adding reactors onto the Barracks to pump out Marines faster. Throw down several depots to avoid supply blocks, and then take your next expansion with a fresh Command Center.

[Step 8] Ramp up production by training a few more Marines, a Medivac, and a Viking for anti-air, while pumping out a pair of Siege Tanks to solidify your ground force; add a handful of Supply Depots to keep the growing army supplied without getting supply blocked.

[Step 9] Continue marine production with 5 marines, then add a siege tank from the factory and a medivac from the starport. Depots go down to keep supply flowing, followed by a second tank to round out the composition.

[Step 10] Add a depot to keep supply flowing while pumping out a wave of Marines from the Barracks for early game pressure or defense, then throw down another depot to ensure you don't get supply blocked as you continue unit production.

[Step 11] Morph the Command Center into an Orbital Command for extra scans and MULEs, while your Starport churns out two Medivacs and the Factory produces a pair of Siege Tanks. Back at the Barracks, reinforce with a wave of Marines to round out your bio-mech army.

[Step 12] Continue producing Marines from the barracks while adding a couple of Siege Tanks from the Factory and a Viking from the Starport to round out a solid bio-mech army, ready to apply pressure or defend against incoming threats.

[Step 13] Crank out a wave of Marines from your Barracks, using all available resources to boost your army count.

[Step 14] Produce two Siege Tanks from the Factory while pumping out a wave of Marines from the Barracks to reinforce the army.

[Step 15] Continue producing a wave of Marines from the Barracks while adding a Siege Tank from the Factory, then throw down a few extra depots to free up supply for the growing army.

[Step 16] Throw down a wave of supply depots to avoid getting blocked, produce a Siege Tank from the Factory, and reinforce with a few Marines from the Barracks to round out your bio-mech composition.

[Step 17] Start producing a wave of Marines from the rax, and add a Siege Tank from the factory to reinforce for a timing push.

[Step 18] Pump out a batch of marines from the barracks to reinforce your bio army, and add a medivac for drops along with a viking to secure air control or provide vision.

[Step 19] Pump out a wave of Marines while adding a Siege Tank and a Medivac to build a solid bio-tank force for your next push.

[Step 20] Produce a Medivac from the Starport while pumping out a wave of Marines from the Barracks to reinforce your army.

[Step 21] Throw down a series of depots to free up supply while continuing to pump marines from the Barracks—start with one marine, add four depots, then train another marine, one more depot, and finish with four more marines to keep your army growing.

[Step 22] Ramp up marine production significantly by queuing a full wave of 14 Marines to build a strong bio army for an upcoming push.

[Step 23] A methodical bio-mech timing that builds relentless pressure from early tanks into a mid-game deathball, leveraging sustained production and defensive positioning to overwhelm opponents through sheer volume. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline -- adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
