# Summary

Open with a Reaper expand off one gas, drop a quick 2nd CC while teching into a Factory and Starport with tech labs, then produce a Raven for early safety and map vision. Mid-game you slam down a third base and start massing up on 5-7 rax with reactors, grabbing Stim, Combat Shield, and +1 infantry, while mixing in a few Tanks and Vikings from the Factory and Starport for a solid bio-mech punch. Late-game you keep pumping Marines non-stop while adding an Armory for +1 vehicle weapons and churning out Siege Tanks and Vikings to max out on a Marine-Tank-Viking deathball, aiming to overwhelm with constant production and mobility.

# Details

[Step 1] Start by training a few SCVs while throwing down a Supply Depot and the first Refinery. Follow up with a Barracks, add a second Refinery, and continue worker production to keep your economy humming for early tech.

[Step 2] Morph the first Command Center into an Orbital Command while starting the Factory and continuing Reaper production from the Barracks. Keep SCV production constant from the main base, add a depot for supply, and produce a couple of Hellions from the Factory. Once your resources allow, throw down a second Command Center for an expansion and reinforce your army with more Reapers while maintaining steady worker production.

[Step 3] With the first Starport and tech labs on both the Factory and Barracks finishing, keep SCV production going while starting to build up your mech force — train a Cyclone and a Siege Tank from the Factory, and a Raven from the Starport for detection and support. Add another Refinery for gas, and morph your Command Center into an Orbital Command to boost income and scanning capability.

[Step 4] Drop another Command Center to take a third expansion, while keeping constant SCV production from your main and natural. Add a pair of depots to manage supply, train a Raven from the Starport for detection and harassment, and throw a Reactor on the Barracks to ramp up Marine production.

[Step 5] Keep SCV production humming while dropping a pair of depots to avoid supply blocks, and reinforce the army with a Siege Tank and a Viking before adding another Barracks to ramp up marine production.

[Step 6] Keep SCV production steady while throwing down three additional Barracks and upgrading the main Command Center to an Orbital for extra scans and income. Start producing a Siege Tank for defensive firepower and a pair of Vikings to secure map vision or deal with enemy air threats.

[Step 7] Drop an Engineering Bay to kick off upgrades, take three Refineries to secure gas income, put a Reactor on your Barracks for double Marine output, and keep workers flowing from your Command Centers.

[Step 8] Start researching Stim from a Tech Lab Barracks and begin +1 infantry weapons from the Engineering Bay, while adding a Reactor onto the second Barracks to ramp up Marine production. Throw down a supply depot to avoid being supply blocked and train a Viking from the Starport for map vision and anti-air support. Keep SCV production steady across all bases.

[Step 9] Continue worker production while reinforcing with a Viking from the Starport and a Siege Tank from the Factory, throw down several supply depots to avoid being supply blocked, and research infantry armor to keep your bio units durable.

[Step 10] Throw down four more rax to ramp up bio production, while continuously churning out SCVs to support the expanding economy. Then produce a Siege Tank from the factory for early pressure or defense.

[Step 11] Keep dropping depots to avoid supply blocks while reinforcing with a handful of Marines and a Medivac for early drop or healing support.

[Step 12] Build the first Armory to open up vehicle upgrades, then pump several Marines from your Barracks while adding a reactor to one of them for faster production. Keep supply open with a depot and start producing a pair of Medivacs to support the bio force.

[Step 13] Add a few depots to free up supply, then attach a Reactor onto one of your Barracks to double Marine production. Keep the Factory working on a Siege Tank while training several Marines from the reactored Barracks to bolster your force.

[Step 14] While continuing to train several Marines and researching Combat Shield to buff the bio army, throw down two additional depots to avoid supply blocks, start upgrading vehicle weapons for mech viability, and produce a Liberator to gain map control or contain the opponent.

[Step 15] Research infantry +1 attack for your bio, throw down several depots to keep supply open, and reinforce with 3 marines while producing a Siege Tank for defensive positioning.

[Step 16] Pump out a dozen Marines from your rax to build up the bio count, drop an extra depot to keep supply flowing, and start the infantry armor upgrade to make the marines more durable.

[Step 17] Drop a Factory and start producing your first Siege Tank while the Starport builds a Viking; keep the Rax churning out a steady wave of Marines, and add another Depot to stay ahead on supply.

[Step 18] Pump out a wave of Marines while adding a couple of Supply Depots to avoid getting supply blocked, keeping the pressure on with a steady bio stream.

[Step 19] Pump out a solid wave of marines from the barracks while rounding out the composition with a Siege Tank and a pair of Vikings for anti-air and map control.

[Step 20] Keep pumping Marines for early map presence and defense, then throw down a Tech Lab on the Factory to unlock higher-tech options while adding a Reactor to the Barracks to double up Marine production.

[Step 21] Continue pumping out marines in waves while adding a pair of vikings for air control and a single siege tank for positional strength, then kick off vehicle weapons to boost your mech firepower.

[Step 22] This is a safe, macro-oriented bio-mech opening that uses an early Raven for detection and map control, then snowballs into a relentless Marine-Tank-Viking deathball through constant production and upgrades. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline -- adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
