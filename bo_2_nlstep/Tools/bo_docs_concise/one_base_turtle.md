# Summary

Opener goes CC first into orbital, stays on one base with a bunker rush at the natural, and rushes a factory with tech lab for siege tanks while spamming marines from a single rax. Mid-game sees nonstop marine production from that rax and a second tech-lab rax for marauders, with siege tanks rolling out of the factory; no expansion is taken, and the whole army is built for a slow, turtled-up push off one base. Late-game the one-base economy caps the army at a small marine-tank ball, with no tech switch or upgrades mentioned, aiming to walk across the map and siege down the opponent before they can outmacro you.

# Details

[Step 1] Keep SCVs pumping nonstop from the main base, throw down a depot early to avoid supply blocks, then start building the first rax and a refinery. Add a second depot as needed, and train the first marine once the rax finishes to provide a bit of early defense.

[Step 2] Morph the Command Center into an Orbital Command for MULEs and scans, while keeping SCV production going and training a few Marines for early defense. Start the Factory to begin the tech transition, throw down a Supply Depot for population, and build a Bunker at the natural for safety along with a second Bunker to reinforce the front. Also drop a Refinery for additional gas income to support Factory tech.

[Step 3] Transition into a Siege Tank push by building a Factory with a Tech Lab, then immediately start producing a Tank while keeping SCV and Marine production going. Throw down a Supply Depot to keep up with supply and drop a second Barracks with a Tech Lab to set up for Marauder tech or additional marine production.

[Step 4] Add the second Barracks and start producing Siege Tanks from the Factory while keeping Marine production going. Throw down a couple of Supply Depots to manage the growing army.

[Step 5] Continue reinforcing with Marines out of the barracks while your factory produces Siege Tanks; make sure to throw down an extra Supply Depot to keep the production flowing without getting supply blocked.

[Step 6] Keep churning out Marines from the Barracks for a strong bio core while the Factory produces its first Siege Tank, and throw down a couple of depots to avoid getting supply blocked.

[Step 7] Keep churning out marines from the barracks while adding siege tanks from the factory, building up a solid marine-tank force for an upcoming push.

[Step 8] Keep the rax churning out Marines while the factory adds a couple of Siege Tanks, and throw down an extra depot to keep supply from blocking your army buildup.

[Step 9] Continue massing marines from the rax while the factory pumps out two siege tanks to solidify the army for a powerful mid-game push.

[Step 10] Throw down depots to keep supply flowing while massing up a wave of Marines from the Barracks, and pump out a couple of Siege Tanks from the Factory to establish a strong marine-tank army for the upcoming push.

[Step 11] This is a hyper-committed one-base turtle-all-in that leverages bunkers and siege tanks to secure a slow, methodical marine-tank deathball off a single base. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline -- adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
