# Summary

Flying Rust opens with a standard rax-first into quick expansion, grabbing gas early and sequencing into Factory then Starport while dropping a bunker to hold the fort against early aggression. The build scales hard into multi-rax production with a tech-labbed Factory for tanks and dual tech-lab Starports, grabbing Combat Shields as the first key upgrade while flooding marines and beginning the BC queue to establish a bio-mech foundation that can pivot skyward. By the late game the economy supports three bases with multiple OCs, the army morphs into a beefy marine-tank core reinforced by a swelling BC fleet, and the endgame plan is to leverage that sustained production to max out and roll over the opponent with a deathball that has both the ground siege to control chokes and the Yamato-backed air fleet to force game-ending engagements.

# Details

[Step 1] Keep worker production rolling non-stop while throwing down the first rax and a geyser to start gas income. Drop a couple depots to stay ahead of supply, setting up the standard opening for a bio or tech transition.

[Step 2] Morph your first CC into an orbital, then drop a second CC in your main to start the expo timing. Keep worker production rolling and throw down the first refinery to unlock gas. Add a factory for the tech transition and build a depot to stay ahead of supply, while pumping out a wave of marines to hold the fort.

[Step 3] Throw down the first starport and a bunker for defense while morphing the CC into an orbital, then add a second rax and grab the second geyser. Keep worker production rolling with several SCVs and train a few marines to hold the front, then slap a tech lab onto the factory to start sieging up your tech path.

[Step 4] Throw down the fusion core and add a tech lab to the starport to unlock BC tech, while sneaking in a third refinery for gas. Keep worker production rolling with several SCVs and reinforce the front with a few marines, then add another tank to the mech ball to keep up the pressure.

[Step 5] Keep the worker production rolling with several SCVs while throwing down a depot to clear supply block. Pump out 4 Marines from the rax to build up a small bio force, setting up for early map pressure or defense depending on what scouting reveals.

[Step 6] Queue up a BC from the starport to keep the air fleet growing, then throw down 4 depots to clear supply block and make room for more units. Keep worker production rolling with a couple SCVs, add another tank from the factory for siege support, and train a few marines to round out the ground army.

[Step 7] Keep worker production rolling with several SCVs while training a wave of Marines out of the rax to build up a bio ball. Throw down a few depots to stay ahead of supply, then get the first Siege Tank rolling out of the factory to add some heavy firepower to the army.

[Step 8] Throw down a second rax and mass depots to clear supply block, then start a BC out of the fusion core while adding a tech lab and reactor to your raxes. Build a second port with a tech lab, pump a few marines, and queue up a tank to round out your ground forces as you transition into heavy air.

[Step 9] Research combat shields while throwing down 4 more rax to flood the production tab, then reinforce with several Marines and queue up a tank from the factory to give the bio ball some siege support.

[Step 10] Queue up a pair of BCs from the starport while throwing down three depots to clear supply block, then reinforce with four marines and add a tank from the factory to help anchor your ground presence while the air fleet comes online.

[Step 11] Dump all rax production into a massive wave of marines, flooding out units to reinforce your army and prepare for a major push or to solidify your defensive line.

[Step 12] Keep marine production rolling while throwing down three depots to clear supply block, then queue up a tank from the factory and start your first BC at the starport. Drop the third CC to secure your economy as you transition into the late-game air fleet.

[Step 13] Queue up a BC from the starport while massing a wave of marines from the rax, then throw down a couple depots to clear up supply cap before continuing marine production.

[Step 14] Keep marine production rolling while queuing up a BC and a tank to reinforce the army, then throw down a bunker for defensive positioning at the front. Morph another OC to keep the economy humming, and start a second BC so the starport stays busy.

[Step 15] Push out a wave of Marines from the rax while queuing up a tank from the factory—this is the core of your bio-mech reinforcement cycle. Keep the marine production rolling to replace any losses and maintain map pressure, with the siege tank adding the backbone your army needs to control key chokes and force favorable engagements.

[Step 16] Keep the rax pumping with a wave of Marines while queuing up a BC from the starport, then add a tank from the factory and throw down a depot to keep supply ahead of the production curve.

[Step 17] Continue marine production while throwing down several depots to support the growing army, and queue up 2 BCs from the starport to build out the late-game air fleet.

[Step 18] This is a macro-heavy bio-mech transition into sky Terran, starting with a standard economic opening that snowballs into relentless marine-tank production while slowly building an unstoppable BC fleet in the background. The DNA is patient and greedy—establish a fortified economic foundation, then max out with a hybrid deathball that grinds opponents down through sustained multi-pronged pressure before the Yamato fleet seals the game.
