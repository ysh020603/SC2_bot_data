# Summary

The build opens with a standard rax-first into quick natural expansion, getting an orbital on the main CC while throwing down a bunker to hold the front, then transitioning into a Factory with tech lab and Starport with tech lab for Raven-Tank-Liberator tech. Production scales up with a second Starport on reactor, massing Vikings and Liberators for air control while mixing in Marines from a reactor-boosted rax, eventually adding extra Factories and an E-Bay for +1 bio attack as the third base comes online. The late-game aims for a sprawling 4-5 base economy with Armory unlocking vehicle and ship upgrades, culminating in a mixed bio-mech deathball of Marines, Hellions, Tanks, Liberators, and Vikings that overwhelms with sustained production and sky control.

# Details

[Step 1] Keep worker production rolling non-stop while throwing down the first rax and a Refinery to get gas flowing. Add a couple depots to stay ahead of supply, setting up the standard opening for a bio or tech transition.

[Step 2] Morph your first CC into an orbital, then grab a reaper for early scout pressure and map info. Drop a bunker at the front to hold off any aggression while you take your natural expansion behind it. Keep worker production rolling, add a refinery to start mining gas, and throw down a depot to avoid supply block while training a couple of marines to fill that bunker.

[Step 3] Throw down the first factory and attach a tech lab to it while keeping marine production rolling and training a few SCVs. Morph the CC into an orbital and add a second refinery to support the upcoming tech transition.

[Step 4] Keep workers flowing constantly while adding a third Refinery to support the growing gas demands, then queue up another tank from the factory and reinforce with a few Marines from the rax. Throw down a couple of depots to avoid supply block as production ramps up.

[Step 5] Throw down the first starport and start massing depots to clear supply block, dropping four depots back-to-back while keeping SCV production rolling. Train another marine and continue pumping workers to keep the economy humming as the starport comes online.

[Step 6] Keep worker production rolling with several SCVs while adding a tech lab to the starport and a reactor to the rax for faster unit output. Train a marine and a tank to reinforce the army, then start a raven for detection and map control.

[Step 7] Throw down the first starport to open up air production while keeping the worker pipeline flowing with several SCVs. Start churning out a tank from the factory for defensive positioning and siege potential, then add a couple of depots to stay ahead of supply block. Finish by training a marine to reinforce the bio line as the tech transition gets rolling.

[Step 8] Throw down three depots to clear supply block and support the growing army, then add a second starport with a reactor while churning out another raven and a tank. Keep worker production rolling with several SCVs and reinforce with a couple of marines to hold the front.

[Step 9] Throw down the first starport and immediately queue up a raven for detection and harassment support, while the factory continues pumping out a tank for siege line defense. Keep marine and SCV production rolling, add a reactor to the starport to enable faster lib production, and start a liberator for map control and zone denial. Top off the supply with a depot so you don't get blocked as the production ramps up.

[Step 10] Throw down a starport with a reactor while keeping worker production and marine training rolling. Add a tank from the factory for ground control, then queue up a liberator to start establishing air presence. Drop a few depots to stay ahead of supply and keep the marine pump flowing for a solid bio backbone.

[Step 11] Throw down the factory and get a reactor on the starport while training up a wave of marines. Add a couple depots to keep supply ahead and start cranking out a siege tank and a liberator to establish some serious firepower for the mid-game push.

[Step 12] Pump out a Liberator and a wave of Vikings from the starport to seize air control and siege key positions, then throw down a tech lab on the factory for tank or mine tech, and spam several depots to clear up the supply block and keep production flowing smoothly.

[Step 13] Throw down a wave of depots to clear supply block and make room for the air army, then queue up 4 vikings from the starport to establish air superiority and counter any colossus or brood lord threats heading your way.

[Step 14] Flood the starport with a massive wave of vikings while mixing in a few marines from the rax, and slip in a raven for detection and interference support as you prepare to siege the sky and establish air dominance.

[Step 15] Throw down a couple of factories to transition into heavier mech production, queue up a wave of marines from the rax, and start cranking out vikings from the starport to contest air control. Add a tank with a factory tech lab to give your army some siege power, setting up a solid mid-game push with mixed bio and mech support.

[Step 16] Throw down an e-bay and start massing Command Centers to spread across the map, aiming for a fourth and fifth base. Swap the factory onto a reactor while adding a couple of marines and a tank to hold the ground, and queue up a liberator from the starport to help secure airspace and zone enemy armies.

[Step 17] Keep marine production rolling while adding a lib for air control and cranking out a wave of hellions from the factory. Take your third CC and throw down a depot to stay ahead on supply, then start bio +1 attack to keep your infantry relevant into the mid-game.

[Step 18] Throw down an armory to unlock vehicle and ship upgrades, then queue up a lib from the starport for map control. Keep marine production ticking at the rax while cranking out a wave of hellions from the factory, and toss down another depot to stay ahead on supply.

[Step 19] This is a macro-oriented bio-mech hybrid that leverages Raven-Liberator-Tank core tech to establish air dominance and siege control, snowballing into a sprawling multi-base deathball with sustained production across all axes. The build prioritizes economy and tech scaling over early aggression, aiming to overwhelm with a mixed-force deathball backed by sky control in the mid-to-late game. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline -- adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
