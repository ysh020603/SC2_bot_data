# Summary

Rusty Screams opens with a standard 1-1-1 into fast expand, dropping the natural CC on the low ground while getting gas early for a Factory into Starport tech path with a Tech Lab on each to unlock Siege Tank and Banshee. The mid-game scales hard with multiple rax, a second Starport, and Reactor/Tech Lab spread to flood Marines while mixing Banshees for map pressure and Tanks for the frontline, grabbing Combat Shield and pushing out a third CC to bank for a big bio-mech timing. The late-game aims for a maxed-out 3-base deathball with a massive Marine core backed by constant Tank and Banshee production, using overwhelming sustained production to crack open the opponent rather than teching to BCs or Ghosts.

# Details

[Step 1] Throw down a depot and keep worker production rolling with several SCVs, then get your first rax up alongside a refinery to start gas collection. Drop a second depot to keep supply ahead as you continue churning out workers, setting up a clean standard opening for whatever tech path you want to pursue.

[Step 2] Morph the first CC into an orbital and drop a second CC at the natural to set up the expansion, then throw down the first Refinery to start gas income. Keep workers and marines flowing non-stop, then add a Factory to begin the tech transition into mech or a bio-mech mix, and top it off with a depot to stay ahead of supply.

[Step 3] Throw down a starport and a bunker to shore up defense, then morph the main CC into an orbital as it finishes. Keep worker production rolling and add a second rax for more marine output. Meanwhile, attach a tech lab to the factory and the starport to unlock siege tank and banshee tech.

[Step 4] Keep the worker production rolling with several SCVs while training a pair of Marines to hold the front. Add a tank from the factory and queue up a banshee from the starport to start building your harassment potential. Throw down a depot to keep supply ahead of your growing army.

[Step 5] Keep worker production rolling while training up a wave of Marines and a Siege Tank to shore up your frontline. Throw down four depots to clear up supply block as your army grows, setting up a solid defensive posture before you push out.

[Step 6] Add a second rax and throw down a tech lab and reactor on your Barracks to unlock bio upgrades and double marine production, while also putting up a second starport and a third refinery to fuel your air transition. Keep worker production rolling with several SCVs and continue building out your banshee count for map pressure.

[Step 7] Keep worker production rolling while throwing down depots to stay ahead of supply, then add a third Refinery to fuel your tech. Produce a Siege Tank from the factory and a Banshee from the starport, add a tech lab to the port for follow-up, and train a few Marines to round out your army.

[Step 8] Queue up combat shield research while training a wave of Marines to reinforce the bio ball, then add a Banshee from the starport for harassment potential and throw down two depots to keep supply clear for the incoming units.

[Step 9] Keep the rax pumping with a wave of marines while the port cranks out a banshee and the factory adds a tank to the army. Throw down a couple depots to stay ahead of supply, then reinforce with several more marines to build up a solid bio core.

[Step 10] Keep the marine ball growing with several more marines while queuing up a pair of banshees from the starport for map pressure and harassment. Add a tank from the factory to anchor your push, and throw down a couple depots to stay ahead of supply block as production ramps up.

[Step 11] Keep the rax rolling with a steady stream of marines while throwing down a couple depots to stay ahead on supply, then queue up your first tank from the factory to start building that core mech backbone.

[Step 12] Throw down a depot to keep supply ahead while the starport churns out a couple of Banshees for map pressure. Flood out a wave of Marines from the rax to build up a solid bio ball, then add two more depots as the army grows to prevent getting supply blocked.

[Step 13] Keep marine production rolling while adding another tank from the factory and a banshee from the starport to diversify your threat. Throw down two depots to stay ahead of supply, then add a third rax to really crank up bio output. This phase is about building a critical mass of infantry while mixing in air and armor for a multi-pronged push.

[Step 14] Throw down a second rax to bolster production while the starport cranks out another banshee for harassment. Queue up a tank from the factory to anchor your position, then flood out a wave of marines to reinforce your army and prepare for a push.

[Step 15] Take a third CC while keeping marine production rolling out of the rax and adding a pair of banshees from the starport. Queue up a tank from the factory to hold the front, then reinforce with a wave of marines to support the push.

[Step 16] Flood out a wave of Marines from your rax while throwing down a depot to keep supply ahead of the curve, then queue up a Siege Tank from the factory to give your bio some real punch as you prep for the mid-game push.

[Step 17] Keep the marine production rolling non-stop from the rax while churning out a couple of banshees from the starport for harassment potential. Throw down a depot and then quickly add two more depots to stay ahead of supply as the army swells. Make an orbital on the expansion CC to keep the macro flowing with extra mule energy and scans.

[Step 18] This is a classic bio-mech snowball that overwhelms through sustained production and relentless pressure across all phases, leaning on a massive Marine core backed by Tank anchoring and Banshee harassment to grind down opponents through sheer volume rather than teching into spellcasters or battlecruisers. Use this gameplan as your strategic baseline -- adapt your decisions based on what you scout and how the game unfolds.
